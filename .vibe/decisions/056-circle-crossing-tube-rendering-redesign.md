# circle-crossing tube rendering: per-edge Cartesian channels, not a per-cell polar hub

**Context:** ADR 055 introduced `circle-crossing` bridge generation and a first tube renderer modeled directly on the rectangular `computeTubeSegments`: a small polar "hub" per cell (an inset window in local radius/angle space) plus per-side arms. In practice this proved unreliable across three follow-up rounds — the door a cell drew on its outward side and the door its child drew on its own inward side used two independently-computed angular widths that silently disagreed whenever the two rings had different sector counts; fixing that alignment still left the door's *linear* size drifting with radius within a same-sector-count band, since the angular half-width was derived from the ring's own angular step rather than its physical size; and even after both were fixed, channels reaching a bare, unrounded cell "center" at every turn produced a visually noisy result — sharp kinks and crossing lines at nearly every cell, since the rectangular hub model's implicit corner-rounding (arms meeting at a small hub's *corners*, not a single point) doesn't carry over to a topology where neighboring cells are different sizes.

**Decision:** Replace the per-cell polar hub with a per-**edge** Cartesian model:
- Every open connection (tangential/cw, or radial/outward) is its own constant-width channel drawn directly between the two cells' real centers (`computeCircleCellCenter`) — a pair of concentric arcs offset `±TUBE_HALF_WIDTH_RATIO` from the ring's own mid-radius for a same-ring (tangential) edge (the exact offset of a circular arc is another circular arc, no approximation), and a pair of Cartesian-parallel lines offset perpendicular to the center-to-center line for a cross-ring (radial) edge (exact regardless of whether the child sits at the same angle as its parent).
- Closed sides are not reimplemented — `computeCircleTubeSegments` reuses `computeCircleMazeSegments`'s already-correct thin-wall geometry outright (entrance/exit gaps, ring-boundary and hub arcs, everything), adding only a matching stub channel connecting the entrance/exit cell to the maze's own boundary.
- A small constant-radius "hub" circle (four quarter-arcs, pure Cartesian, independent of sector count or rotation) is drawn at every node that terminates at least one channel, rounding what would otherwise be a bare kink at every turn or branch into a consistent joint — giving the maze a "beads and pipes" look distinct from the rectangular tube's smooth flowing corridors, but internally consistent and free of the alignment class of bug ADR 055's design kept reintroducing.
- At a crossing node (see ADR 055), every one of its connections still gets a channel, but the *under* axis's two channels each stop `CROSSING_GAP_FRACTION` (0.35) short of the crossing's own center instead of reaching it, and **no hub circle is drawn at a crossing node** — drawing one would visually plug the gap the under axis relies on to read as "tunneling underneath." The *over* axis's channel(s) reach the center normally, exactly like an ordinary node.

**Reason:** Every one of the follow-up bugs traced back to the same root cause — a per-cell polar model that let two adjacent cells independently compute what should be one shared piece of geometry (a door's width and position), which only stays trivially consistent in the rectangular case because every cell is the same size. Moving to per-**edge** channels computed once, directly between two known points, makes agreement structural rather than something that needs re-deriving and re-testing at every ring transition — the width guarantee ("two lines/arcs exactly `2×TUBE_HALF_WIDTH_RATIO` apart") is now provable from the offset formula itself instead of empirically bounded. This was a genuine redesign rather than another incremental patch, per explicit user direction after the third size-mismatch report.

**Rejected alternatives:**
- Continuing to patch the per-cell polar hub (e.g. deriving the angular half-width from radius instead of angular step, which fixed the *linear width* symptom but not the turn-kink problem): rejected after it became clear the underlying per-cell independence was the actual defect, not any single formula within it.
- Precise per-corner tangent-circle rounding (mirroring the rectangular renderer's `roundCorner`/`roundRealCorners`, see ADR 030/031) instead of a uniform hub circle: would produce a closer visual match to the rectangular tube's rounded-square hubs, but requires computing an arc tangent to two channels of potentially different directions and widths at a shared point in a topology where "the corner" isn't a fixed local shape the way a rectangular cell's is; a fixed-radius hub circle achieves the same practical goal (no bare kinks) far more simply, at the cost of a distinct (not identical) visual style from `rectangle-crossing`.
